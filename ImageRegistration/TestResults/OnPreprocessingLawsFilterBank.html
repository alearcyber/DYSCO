Here are some of the things law did for preprocessing his filter banks



Conversion to Grayscale

This step ensures that the image contains only intensity information, which is relevant for texture analysis



Resize and Padding

The grayscale image is optionally resized to a fixed size or aspect ratio to ensure consistency across different images. Additionally, padding may be applied to the image to obtain a square image, which simplifies the filter bank computations. The padding can be done by extending the image edges or filling with appropriate values



Local Contrast Normalization

Law applied local contrast normalization to the grayscale image. This step helps to enhance the texture details and reduce the influence of overall illumination variations. Local contrast normalization can be performed by subtracting the local mean from each pixel and dividing by the local standard deviation. It ensures that the image has a consistent contrast distribution across different regions.



Centering

The normalized image is centered by subtracting the global mean value of the image from each pixel. This step helps to eliminate any global intensity variations and ensures that the filter bank response is primarily based on local texture variations.
